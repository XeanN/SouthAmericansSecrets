Unlike the Huaca del Sol, which suffered significant destruction over time, the Huaca de la Luna remains largely intact and has yielded a wealth of valuable archaeological information. Within its walls, numerous murals can be found, many of which depict the Moche deity "Ayapec," also known as the "all-knowing" god. These murals offer a glimpse into the religious beliefs and ceremonial practices of the ancient Moche people. Archaeological evidence suggests that the Huaca de la Luna served not only as a ceremonial center but also as a burial site for high-ranking priests and rulers. Additionally, it was a key location for performing human sacrifices, further emphasizing its importance in Moche society's spiritual and political life.
