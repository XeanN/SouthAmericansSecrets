The Chauchilla Cemetery was discovered in the 1920s, though it hasn't been used since the 9th century. This ancient burial site dates back between 600 and 700 years, starting around 200 AD, and provides significant insight into the Nazca culture. Thanks to the extremely dry climate and Nazca burial practices, the mummies are remarkably well-preserved, with many still retaining their hair and skin. Chauchilla offers a fascinating glimpse into ancient customs, making it a highly recommended destination for those with an interest in archaeology and history.
