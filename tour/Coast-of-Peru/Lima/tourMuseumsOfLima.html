These two museums showcase unparalleled pre-Colombian artifacts from Peru and the New World, making them among the most important in South America. The Larco Museum, located in a unique 17th-century colonial residence built atop an 8th-century pyramid, features an exquisite collection of ancient Inca gold and silver, along with a pre-Hispanic erotic art salon. With over 45,000 archaeological pieces, classified by experts, the museum regularly tours major world exhibitions. Larco is one of the few museums worldwide that opens its vaults to the public and boasts the largest private collection of pre-Colombian Peruvian art.
