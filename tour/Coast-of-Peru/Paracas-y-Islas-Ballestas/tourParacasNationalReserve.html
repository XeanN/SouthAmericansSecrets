Due to exceptional biodiversity from the Humboldt Current and year-round coastal upwelling, this part of the Pacific Ocean is one of the most productive in the world. As a wildlife refuge, the reserve hosts 224 bird species, over 180 fish species, and 20 cetaceans. It also boasts 114 archaeological sites, showcasing the ancient inhabitants' successful interaction with the sea. Declared a Ramsar Site in 1991, Paracas National Reserve offers stunning geological features, panoramic views, and pristine white sand beaches lined with colorful sedimentary rocks and turquoise waters.
